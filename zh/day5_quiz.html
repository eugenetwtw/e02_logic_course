Review Points

  1. Inductive Reasoning: From specific to general, the conclusion is only probably true, not necessarily true
  2. Argument by Analogy: Reasoning based on similarities, evaluating its strength requires considering the number and relevance of similarities
  3. Causal Reasoning: Reasoning about causal relationships between events, avoiding Post Hoc Fallacy and Correlation Fallacy
  4. Mill's Methods: Agreement, Difference, Concomitant Variation, Residues, and Joint Method, used to determine causal relationships
  5. Critical Thinking: Identifying problems, gathering information, evaluating evidence, considering alternative explanations, forming conclusions
  6. Scientific Method: Observation, question, hypothesis, prediction, testing, analysis, conclusion, repetition
  7. Good Scientific Hypothesis: Falsifiability, explanatory power, simplicity, consistency, testability
