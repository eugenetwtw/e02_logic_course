1. The characteristic of Deductive Reasoning is:

  • A. The term that serves as the predicate in the conclusion
  • B. The term that serves as the subject in the conclusion
  • C. The term that appears in both premises but not in the conclusion

5. The "Modus Ponens" rule is:

  • A. If p → q is true, and p is true, then q is true
  • B. If p → q is true, and q is false, then p is false
  • C. If p → q is true, and q → r is true, then p → r is true
  • D. If p ∨ q is true, and ¬p is true, then q is true

6. The "Modus Tollens" rule is:

  • A. If p → q is true, and p is true, then q is true

Review Points

  1. Deductive Reasoning: From general to specific, if the premises are true, then the conclusion must be true
  2. Syllogism: Consists of major premise, minor premise, and conclusion, involving major term, middle term, and minor term
  3. Logical symbols: ∀ (universal quantifier), ∃ (existential quantifier), ∧ (AND), ∨ (OR), ¬ (NOT), → (IF-THEN), ↔ (IF AND ONLY IF)
  4. Basic inference rules: Modus Ponens, Modus Tollens, Hypothetical Syllogism, Disjunctive Syllogism, Addition, Simplification, Conjunction
  5. Venn diagrams: Used to represent set relationships and analyze the validity of syllogisms
